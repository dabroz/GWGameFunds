## Legend

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Class** | Gamalytic scale tier of the publisher's portfolio: Indie / AA / AAA |
| **Budget `$`** | Typical project budgets: `$` = under ~$250K · `$$` = ~$250K–$1M · `$$$` = $1M+ |
| **Comm.** | Communication responsiveness rating from the source sheet: ★ (slow) to ★★★ (responsive) · `?` = no data |
| **Funding type** | *Publishing* = publishing deal (rev share) · *Project* = project investment, dev keeps IP · *Equity* = company investment · *Grant* = non-dilutive |
| `[?]` | Unverified or estimated data |
| `—` | Data genuinely unavailable |
| Rev figures | Lifetime Steam revenue, Gamalytic estimate (per source sheet, 2026) |

## How to Use This Directory

1. **Match genre & budget first.** Read a publisher's last ~10 releases before pitching. A misaligned pitch signals you didn't do your homework.
2. **Know what you're trading.** Publishers take a revenue share and handle marketing/porting; VC funds take equity in your company; developer funds usually let you keep IP; grants are non-dilutive but slow and restricted.
3. **Have a vertical slice.** In 2025–2026 paper pitches are largely dead — a polished playable build is the baseline for serious funding conversations.
4. **Verify status.** The 2024–2025 downturn closed or shrank many publishers. Confirm a company is active and open to pitches before investing time.
5. **Check the red flags (⚠️)** — they come from developer reports collected in the source sheet and press. They are leads for due diligence, not verdicts.
